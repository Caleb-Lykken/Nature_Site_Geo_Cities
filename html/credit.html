Modern web dev courses such as CSE 154 focus on using coding to create websites, i.e. in HTML, CSS, and Javascript. Using three different languages for different aspects of the website allows for more complex visual effects, as well as responsive websites and database integration, among other capabilities. Modern website builders using drag-and-drop-type interfaces do exist, but they are primarily websites themselves, rather than being apps that are downloaded onto your system, and they are not taught in modern web dev courses. The modern principles of web user interface design are also different, with a lesser emphasis on personality and exploration and a greater emphasis on usability and professionalism.

We chose the topic of hikes near Seattle since we all like to hike and thought it would be an interesting topic about which to make a themed page. Also, we were able to find some good photos related to it.We sourced images from DiscMaster and GifCities, and we utilized Microsoft Paint and Photopea, as well as an online transparent PNG-GIF converter, in order to resize images and change them to JPG/GIF format so that they could be displayed correctly on our website.

GeoCities users in the past built their websites to fit into a community they were hoping to join, shaping their visual design and what they chose to put on their website. This social aspect of creating a website is one that cannot be replicated in a classroom setting with only a few websites being made.
